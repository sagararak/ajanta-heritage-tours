About Ajanta Caves

Ajanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983, nestled in Maharashtra, India. These 30 rock-cut Buddhist monuments date from the 2nd Century BCE to 480 CE.

They are celebrated for their murals and frescoes illustrating the Jataka tales, and house both Chaitya-grihas (prayer halls) and Viharas (monasteries).

Our exclusive focus on Ajanta ensures you receive the most immersive, educational, and memorable experience.
